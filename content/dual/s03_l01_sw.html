Fasili mbalimbali hazielezi namna gani hali ya kuwa kiziwi asiyeona inavyotokea. Ni kawaida kutofautisha kati ya udhaifu wa kuona na kusikia tangu kuzaliwa na acquired uliojitokeza baadaye katika maisha.

  • Istilahi kiziwi asiyeona wa tangu kuzaliwa hutumika wakati watu wanazaliwa wakiwa viziwi wasioona au endapo udhaifu wao wa kusikia na kuona unapounganika ukitokea hata kabla ya aina za lugha na mawasiliano ya kuongea, ya ishara au aina nyinginezo hazijaibuka.
  • Neno kiziwi asiyeona lilizuka baadaye katika maisha na hutumika endapo watu wanazaliwa wakiwa viziwi au wakiwa wanasikia kwa shida na baadaye katika maisha uono unadhoofika.
    Au endapo watu wanazaliwa wakiwa na udhaifu wa kuona au wakiwa hawaoni, na baadaye katika maisha usikivu hudhoofika.

Sababu za kuwa kiziwi asiyeona zinazojulikana na wote

A variety of known causes for deafblindness exists. These include congenital conditions and a wide variety of conditions that occur or manifest after birth.

The most common conditions are:

  • Prematurity is a birth related conditions. A baby born at less than 37 weeks’ gestation is considered premature. As a general rule, the more premature an infant and the smaller the birth weight, the greater The risk of complications are greater when the infants has an extremely low birth weight (1000 grams). They are more susceptible to possible complications of a premature birth. Some premature infants have ongoing medical problems which could involve the sensory system (including vision and hearing problems). These medical problems could extend from childhood into adulthood. Vision
    It is thought that changes in the oxygen exposure of the infant in the nursery can cause a disruption in the process of the eye developing naturally. This disruption can result in an eye condition called retinopathy of prematurity (ROP). In some cases, may be treatable while in other cases may not be successful. If treatment is not an option or is not successful, then it may lead to retinal detachment and blindness. Infants with ROP are also at a greater risk for having eye conditions such as myopia, strabismus, amblyopia and glaucoma.

    Hearing
    Several things can cause varying degrees of hearing loss in premature infants. In prematurity, the underdeveloped blood vessels of the inner ear can easily hemorrhage. It could decrease the blood supply to the organ of corti, causing irreversible damage to the cells in this area. Other risk factors which can contribute to hearing loss in premature infants include hypoxia, hyperbilirubinemia, or physical trauma at birth.

    Source: Center for Disabilities, University of South Dakota , NHS United Kingdom
  • Inflammation in the brain as corneitis and meningitis. Meningitis is the inflammation of the tissue lining of the brain and spinal cord. There are two broad categories of meningitis; viral and bacterial. The symptoms may include nausea, vomiting, discomfort looking into bright lights, confusion, loss of balance and sleepiness. It can cause vision and cortical visual problems such as deafness, brain damage, or seizures among others. A person who becomes sick with bacterial meningitis needs immediate medical treatment. In rare cases, fungal meningitis can cause dangerous form of meningitis for premature babies. Antibiotics are used to treat bacterial meningitis.
    Source: Texas School for the Blind and Visually Impaired , A-Z to Deafblindness
  • Cerebral palsy is a problem with the brain and nervous system that mainly affects movement and co-ordination. CP may result from the following:
    - Bleeding in the baby’s brain or reduced blood and oxygen supply to the brain.
    - An infection caught by the mother during pregnancy among others
    Birth related causes can be generically inherited in nature. This can be as a result of: chromosomal defects, single gene defects, dominant inheritance, and recessive inheritance.
    Source: NHS United Kingdom, NHS United Kingdom, Boston Childrens Hospital

Eye diseases among deafblind children in East Africa

The most common eye diseases in deafblind children in East Africa are:

  • Cataract. The lenses are unclear. Cataract can lead to glaucoma.
  • Glaucoma. High pressure in the eye. If not treated, can lead to blindness.
  • Coloboma. Cleft in the eye that leads to decreased visual field and visual acuity.
  • Optical nerve atrophy. That leads to decreased visual field and visual acuity.
  • Retina detachment. Leads to total blindness. It is often a consequence of other eye diseases.
  • Cerebral visual impairment. The visual impairment is not in the eye itself but in the nerves leading to to the visual part in the brain, or in the visual brain itself. this condition leads to problems with understanding visual information, problems with sustained visual attention and with sensory integration.

Ear diseases among deafblind children in East Africa

Many different conditions lead to partial and total deafness include ear infections, fluid buildup behind the ear drum, holes in the ear drum, and problems with the middle ear bones that can cause deafness from conductive hearing loss. In rare cases, tumours can also cause conductive hearing loss – they block sound waves from getting into the inner ear.
Birth defects and diseases passed on by genes can do this too. Genetics is one cause of sensorineural hearing loss. Half of all cases of profound deafness in children have a genetic source.

Source: MedBroadcast

Challenges of deafblindness

Deafblindness entails major challenges and may affect all or some of the following:

  • Communication
    This can impair deafblind people during interaction with people around them, sharing knowledge and information; exchanging greetings; conveying needs; and freely expressing their feelings to enable them make informed choices in the environment. Development of formal language is difficult.
  • Access to information, education and employment
    They are always faced with the challenge of getting feedback, making career choices and access to information, if they don’t get the right support
  • Orientation and mobility
    Good orientation and mobility skills is key to giving people independence and the opportunity to maintain a healthy lifestyle. Reduced mobility skills restrict persons with deablindness to access activities of daily living and moving safely to where one wants to go.
  • Learning
    The learning processes can be demanding, depending on individual abilities and the severity of the impairment. For example, following routines during ADLs. Essential information is missed and incidental learning is greatly limited.
  • Social relationships
    Social relationships create social bonds through interaction. Lack of this may lead to behavioral change and challenges in adapting to the new environment. They may detach themselves and avoid interactions.
  • Isolation
    Social skills cannot be learnt in isolation. Positive peer interaction is important. Lack of access to complete visual and auditory information results in varying degrees of isolation and disconnection from the world around them.
  • Confidence and self-esteem
    The challenges of not making new friends, explore new ideas and activities, reach out to help others, take on greater responsibility as they mature.
  • Independence in daily living skills
    Living independently will be difficult or even impossible for learners who are deafblind without receiving some level of support from a partner.
  • Access interpreters and to aids and equipment because of cost
    Accessing support services like sign language/tactile interpretation, or assistive devices at high costs hinders persons with deafblindness from accessing information, and participating fully in day-to-day activities, or even activities of daily living.
  • Injuries in the brain nerves that affect the face muscles can cause paralysis in parts of the face. This can affect mimics, which in turn increases the chance that the child is easily exposed to misunderstandings in communication. This may also affect the possibilities of expressing feelings such as pain, joy, sadness and anger.
  • It is energy-intensive to concentrate when vision, hearing and balance are impaired and there may be frequent breaks and variation in the work tasks.
  • Problems with self-regulation, functions that regulate and control emotions, thoughts and behaviors can lead to behaviors that are perceived as challenging to the environment and are difficult to understand.

Hisia kwa njia ya kugusa

Hisia kwa njia ya kugusa ina umuhimu wa pekee kwa watu wenye matatizo ya kusikia. Hisia ya kugusa imesambaa mwilini kote na kwenye miisho ya neva katika ngozi na sehemu nyingine za mwili ambazo husafirisha hisi hadi kwenye ubongo. Baadhi ya sehemu za mwili ni nyepesi kuhisi zaidi kuliko nyingine. Ncha ya vidole ni miongoni mwa sehemu za mwili zenye idadi kubwa ya mkusanyiko wa miisho ya neva na kwa hiyo kuwa ni yenye umuhimu mkubwa kwa watu ambao ni viziwi wasioona.

Wafundishe wanafunzi ili kutumia hisia ya kugusa.

Sikiliza au soma kile ambacho Mary Maragia anachosema kuhusu hisia ya kugusa.

Hisia ya kugusa kwakweli ni njia ya awali kwa wanafunzi ambao ni viziwi wasioona kwakuwa uwezo wao wa kusikia na kuona umedhoofika. Hivyo wanamazoea ya kutumia hisi nyinginezo, hisia ya kunusa, hisia ya kugusa, hisia saidizi kama vile masazo ya kusikia na masalio ya kuona. Hivyo inapokuja kwenye hisi ya kugusa tunaamini au kujua kwamba kuna neva nyingi, neva za ufahamu katika vidole gumba vyao (ncha za vidole?). Hivyo, bado tunahitaji kuiamsha hisi yao ya kugusa na halafu tunawapatia shughuli mbalimbali ili waweze kuzitofautisha, kuwatofautisha watu, kutofautisha, kama utambulisho wangu, wanajua kuwa wewe ni Maria, wanajua kuwa wewe ni profesa, wanaweza kumjua mtu mwingine. Wana uhodari katika kutambua kuwa jambo hili ni tofauti na jingine. Hivyo dhima yetu kama walimu ni kuweka mazingira wezeshi na kuwapatia fursa. Tunawasaidia kufanya marudio mengi na halafu kuwaimarisha na kuwatia shime wanafunzi. Hivyo tunaelekea kwa hisia tano, hisia kumi, ahaa ajabu.

Hivyo, wanafunzi wanamazoea ya kuhisi – ahaah, mwalimu alinielewa. Hivyo mara tu ninapokuwa na furaha kama mwalimu, ninapochangamana na wanafunzi, mwalimu atajua. Siku ambayo sijisikii vyema, watajua tu kwamba mwalimu leo hii sie yeye, hakuna kitakachofanyika siku hiyo. Lakini mara tu wanapopata – aaaha, mwalimu yuko hapa kwa ajili yangu -, hivyo tena kuwa kiziwi asiyeona ni kukutana kwa kiwango kikubwa. Baadhi ya wanafunzi wanataka sana kushika, hivyo mara wanapojua kuwa upo pale, wanaamini kuwa wako salama. Bila ya mtu kuwa karibu, baadhi yao wataanza kujirusha, kujing’ata, kujipiga wenyewe; kuchokoa macho yao. Hivyo mara tu tunapojiweka kuwa washirika wao au kama walimu tunaweza kufanyakazi nao vyema na wanaacha tabia hiyo na hii tabia ya kujijeruhi.
