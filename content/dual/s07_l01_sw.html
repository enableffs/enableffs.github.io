Uganda

Kwa sasa hakuna sera mahususi iliyokwishapitishwa kuhusu elimu yenye mahitaji maalum kwaajili ya watu ambao vi viziwi wasioona nchini Uganda. Hata hivyo, kuna sera za jumla ambazo husaidia watu wote wenye ulemavu. Sera kuhusu elimu yenye mahitaji maalum bado iko kwenye hatua ya kuwa rasimu.


Serikali ya Uganda imechukua hatua kadhaa za kisheria na kisera zinazoonyesha nia ya kuendeleza haki za watu wenye ulemavu, ikiwa ni pamoja na watu ambao ni viziwi wasioona.

Mapatano na Mikataba katika ngazi ya kimataifa

Kuhusu hati rasmi za kimataifa, hatua hizi ni pamoja na kuweka saini na kuridhia:

  • Mkataba wa Umoja wa Mataifa kuhusu Haki za Watu wenye Ulemavu (CRPD)(CRPD) Kusainiwa na kuridhiwa (mwaka 2008) kwa Mkataba wa Umoja wa Mataifa kuhusu Haki za Watu wenye Ulemavu (CRPD) na itifaki ya hiari, mkataba wa kwanza wa kimataifa, unaobana kisheria wenye lengo la kulinda haki za kibinadamu za watu wenye ulemavu. Ripoti ya kwanza ya nchi kwa Kamati ya Umoja wa Mataifa juu ya Haki za Watu wenye Ulemavu ilitolewa na Uganda mwaka 2013 (ilitarajiwa 2010), ikiwa ni pamoja na mlolongo wa nia kwa ajili ya hatua ya baadaye. Jumuiya za kiraia zimeandaa Taarifa mbadala.
  • Mkataba Unaopiga Marufuku ya Uchimbaji Usainishaji na uridhiaji wa Mkataba wa Marufuku ya Uchimbaji madini mwaka 2001

    • Uganda pia imekwishasaini na kuridhia mikataba mingine inayoendeleza haki za watu, ikiwa ni pamoja na haki za walemavu. Mikataba inarejelea katika ulindaji wa haki za watu wenye ulemavu ili kutendewa bila uonevu, matunzo yanayostahili, ujumuishi na ushiriki kamili katika jamii.

    • Mapatano ya Umoja wa Mataifa juu ya Haki za Mtoto
    • Mkataba wa Afrika juu ya Haki za Kibinadamu na Haki za Watu
    • Mkataba juu ya Kuondoa Aina Zote za Ubaguzi dhidi ya Wanawake

    Uganda imeweka nia yake katika Sera ya Afrika Mashariki kuhusu Watu wenye Ulemavu (2012) ikiainisha nia za pamoja za kisera ikienda sambamba na CRPD na mapendekezo ya kutoka kwenye ngazi ya nchi.

  • Mpango Kazi wa Kitaifa kwaajili ya Kipindi cha Miaka Kumi ya Afrika ya Watu wenye Ulemavu Uganda tayari ilikubali azimio la Mpango Kazi wa Kitaifa kwaajili ya Kipindi cha Miaka Kumi ya Watu wenye Ulemavu mwaka 2005, kwa bajeti ya shilingi za Uganda milioni 1.8. Kamisheni ya Afrika juu ya Haki za Kibinadamu na Haki za Watu imeandaa rasimu Itifaki juu ya Haki za Watu wenye Ulemavu. Ilitolewa kwaajili ya maoni mnamo Aprili 2014 na mara ikikamilishwa, inaelekea kuwa ni sehemu ya mwongozo wa kisera wa Uganda kwaajili ya kuendeleza haki za ulemavu. Hati nyingine muhimu zinazokuja mwaka 2014 ni Kitini cha Afrika cha CRPD naMwongozo wa Uwajibikaji kwaajili ya Afrika ambayo ni sehemu ya Msimamo wa Pamoja wa Afrika Baada ya Kipindi cha Maendeleo hadi kufikia 2015.

Mapatano na mikataba katika ngazi ya kitaifa ya Uganda

Kuhusu sheria na sera, mambo yafuatayo yamekuwa yakiathiri mwelekeo wa haki za watu wenye ulemavu:

  • Katiba ya Uganda ya1995 inatambua haki za watu wenye ulemavu, ikiwa ni pamoja na watu ambao ni viziwi wasioona na inaainisha haki hizi kwa undani kidogo.
    Katiba inatamka kwamba Dola itakuza maendeleo ya lugha ya ishara kwaajili ya viziwi, kutoa elimu kwa wote (ibara ya 20) na hujumlisha uwakilishi wa upendeleo maalum wa watu wenye ulemavu katika Bunge (ibara ya 75). Inaelezea usawa na kupiga marufuku ubaguzi dhidi ya watu wote hususani inajumuisha watu wenye ulemavu (ibara ya 21).
    Inatamka zaidi kwamba Dola ni lazima ifanye upendeleo maalum kwaajili ya makundi yaliyopembezoni (ikiwa ni pamoja na watu wenye ulemavu ambao watu viziwi wasioona wanajumuishwa) na watatunga sheria ili kutimiza sharti hili, ikiwa ni pamoja na sheria za kuanzisha Kamisheni ya Fursa Sawa kwa Wote (ibara ya 32). Zaidi ya hapo, huelezea kuhusu haki za watu wenye ulemavu za heshima na utu wa kibinadamu. Pia inalazimisha nchi kuwa na jukumu la kutunga sheria zinazofaa kwaajili ya ulinzi wa watu wenye ulemavu(ibara ya 35).
  • The Persons with Sheria ya Watu wenye Ulemavu iliyoanza kutekelezwa mwaka 2006 inaweka bayana uhakikisho wa ulinzi wa kisheria na fursa sawa za watu wenye ulemavu, ikijikita katika mtazamo wa haki. Sheria hii pia inaweka wazi uwakilishi wa Watu wenye Ulemavu (PWD) katika vyombo vya kutoa maamuzi (viti vilivyohifadhiwa) katika ngazi zote za kiutawala, ikiwa ni pamoja na mabaraza ya mitaa. Sera ya Taifa ya Ulemavu nchini Uganda 2006 ilikubaliwa ili kuhakikisha utekelezaji wa Sheria ya Ulemavu.
    Mwaka 2009, serikali ilijaribu kubatilisha Sheria ya Ulemavu ya mwaka 2006, kwakuwa baadhi ya sehemu zilikuwa zinahusisha gharama zikiwa zinaruhusu upunguzaji mkubwa wa kodi: asilimia 10 kwa makampuni yenye kuajiri zaidi kuliko watu 15 wenye ulemavu. Kodi ilipunguzwa hadi asilimia 2 na watu wenye ulemavu wakipewa ruzuku maalum ya kila mwaka ya shilingi ya Uganda milioni 30 kwa kila wilaya ili kusaidia maendeleo ya kijamii na kiuchumi na ajira.
    Mnamo Machi 2012, jumuia za kiraia zilizindua Mapendekezo mapana kwaajili ya Marekebisho ya Sheria ya Watu wenye Ulemavu ya 2006 ili kusudi kuhakikisha CRPD ina kuwa ni sehemu ya sheria za nchi kwa ukamilifu wote. Mapendekezo hayo yaliongozwa na Chama cha Kitaifa cha Viziwi cha Uganda (UNAD), Hatua za Kisheria kwaajili ya Watu wenye Ulemavu (LAPD) na Afya ya Akili Uganda (MHU). Mapendekezo bado yanangoja uamuzi.

Sheria na sera nyingine muhimu ni pamoja na:

  • Sheria ya Matibabu ya Akili iliyotungwa mwaka 1938 (iliyofanyiwa masahihisho mwaka 1964) bado inaendelea kutumika. Tukijua kwamba watu ambao ni viziwi wasioona wana udhaifu katika fahamu nyingi, sheria hii itasaidia kuendeleza haki za watu kama hawa
  • Sheria ya Baraza ka Kitaifa la Ulemavu , mwaka 2003 ilianzisha Baraza la Kitaifa la Ulemavu, kazi yake muhimu ni kuwa chombo cha uratibu wa kitaifa kwanjia yake mahitaji, matatizo, mahangaiko ya watu wenye ulemavu yanaweza kufikishwa serikalini na kwa mawakala zake.
  • Sheria ya Ajira , 2006 inaweka wazi ulinzi na usawa wa watu wote kuajiriwa katika maeneo ya kazi ikiwa ni pamoja na watu wenye ulemavu. Sheria hii itasaidia kuendeleza haki za watu ambao ni viziwi wasioona watakapoacha shule ili kujumuishwa katika maisha ya jamii kwa kuunga mkono programu za mpito.
  • Sheria ya Kamisheni ya Fursa Sawa: Kamisheni hii iliteuliwa mwaka 2009 huku mmoja kati ya wajumbe wake watano akiwa mwanamke mwenye ulemavu. Kamisheni hii, ikiwa na sekretarieti iliyokamilika, ilianza kazi yake ya kuendeleza fursa sawa kwa makundi yaliyopembezoni, watu ambao ni viziwi wasioona wakiwemo.
  • Sheria ya Elimu na Mafunzo ya Biashara, Ufundi, na Utaalamu ya 12 ya 2008, huendeleza upatikanaji uliosawa wa elimu na mafunzo kwa makundi yote yaliyokuwa katika hali ngumu, pamoja na walemavu.

Tanzania

The National Policy on Disability aims at providing conducive environment for people with disabilities to engage in productive work for their development and the utilization of available resources for improved service delivery. Currently there is no specifically approved policy on special needs education for the deafblind people in Tanzania. However, Tanzania has adopted and implemented a number of laws, policies and standards pertaining to people with disabilities, including their right to productive and decent work, vocational training and basic services. The 1977 Constitution and its amendments prohibit discrimination against persons with disabilities. The main laws, policies, standards and initiatives are listed below.

Tanzania Mainland:
  • Vocational Education and Training Act 1994 (No. 1), provides a legal framework for the implementation of a flexible vocational education and training system that responds to the labour market.
  • Disabled Persons (Employment) Act 1982 (No. 2). Establishes a quota system which stipulates that 2 per cent of the workforce in companies with over 50 employees must be persons with disabilities. Also establishes the National Advisory Council which advises the minister responsible for social welfare of disabled persons
  • Disabled Persons (Employment) Regulations 1985, defines the eligibility and registration requirements for disabled persons under the Disabled Persons (Employment) Act 1982
  • Disabled Persons (Care and Maintenance) Act 1982 (No. 3), provides and designates responsibilities of caring for disabled persons to families, relatives, local government, central government and non-governmental organizations. Also establishes a National Fund for Disabled Persons.
  • National Policy on Disability 2004, aims at providing a conducive environment for people with disabilities to engage in productive work for their development and the utilization of available resources for improved service delivery.
  • National Employment Promotion Service Act 1999 (No. 9), provides or makes arrangements for the registration, employment, counselling, vocational rehabilitation and placement of persons with disabilities.
  • Employment and Labour Relations Act, 2004 (No. 6), forbids direct and indirect discrimination in any employment policy, including discrimination based on disability.
  • National Strategy for Growth and Reduction of Poverty (MKUKUTA), 2005 to 2010, which recognizes disability as a cause of poverty.

Zanzibar:
  • Disability Policy (2004), views disability as a human rights issue.
  • Zanzibar Labour Act 1997 (No. 3), lays down fundamental rights concerning the employment of people with disabilities.
  • Labour Relations Act 2005 (No. 1), bars discrimination based on disability in the context of Trades Unions’ constitution or activities.
  • The Persons with Disabilities (Rights and Privileges) Act No. 9, 2006.
  • The Persons with Disabilities (Rights and Privileges) Act No. 9, 2006.
  • Workmen’s Compensation Act 1986 (No. 15).
  • The Zanzibar Strategy for Growth and Reduction of Poverty (MKUZA), 2006-2010
  • The Zanzibar Education Policy and its Education Development Program, finalized in late 2007, focuses on improving access to education for children with disabilities.
  • Zanzibar Vision 2020 envisions, among other aims, equal opportunities for orphans, people with disabilities and other disadvantaged groups in every aspect of social, economic and cultural life.

Source: Inclusion of People with Disabilities in the United Republic of Tanzania

Kenya

The United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child (1989), the UN Standard Rules on the Equalization of Opportunities for Persons with Disabilities (1993) and the Salamanca Statement and Framework for Action (1994) advocates for the rights and education for all children including those with special needs. It is their fundamental rights.

The Kenyan policy on Education, was borrowed from within the framework of Education for All, the Millennium Development Goals, Convention on the Rights of the Child and on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities.
