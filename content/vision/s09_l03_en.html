Activities for Daily Living

It is important to be aware that persons with visual impairment have capabilities to perform their activities for daily living. This is made possible with early training in these skills both at home and school. The following are some of activities of daily living which persons with visual impairment can do to fulfil the day to day personal needs:

  • Self-grooming such as washing, using the toilet, shaving, dressing, body hygiene care
  • Independent organizations such as planning and arranging personal items, and time management
  • Social skills like knowledge of socially acceptable habits, communication skills, and norms when meeting people such as greeting, taking turns and level of conversation
  • Family life and home management such as bed-making and orderliness of household items
  • Shopping and food preparation
  • Engaging in income earning activities such as gardening, and poultry keeping
