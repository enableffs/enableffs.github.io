Organizations of people with visual impairment:

African Union of the Blind

The African Union of the Blind (AFUB) is an umbrella organisation of 60 member organisations of and for blind and partially sighted persons in 53 African countries.
In East Africa, Kenya Union of the Blind, Uganda National Association of the Blind and Tanzania League of the Blind are members in the organzation.

Read more about African Union of the Blind


Kenya Union of the Blind (KUB)

Kenya Union of the Blind is a non-political, membership-based NGO that collaborates with the government, corporate and civil society organizations to promote the rights of persons with visual impairment in Kenya. The aim of the organization is to promote the social inclusion of persons with visual impairment through uniting and empowering them as well as advocating for their rights.

Read more about Kenya Union of the Blind (KUB)


Uganda National Association of the Blind (UNAB)

The vision of the organization is to have a self-sustaining association of empowered persons with visual impairment, enjoying equal opportunities in society.
The mission is to better the quality of life of the persons with visual impairment in Uganda, regardless of sex and age through advocacy, networking, capacity building and model service provision.

Read more about Uganda National Association of the Blind (UNAB)


Tanzania League of the Blind

Tanzania League of the Blind is an organization that aims to promote social inclusion for persons with visual impairment in Tanzania. The organization was created and it is 100% led by persons with visual impairment themselves who work on a voluntary basis. The organization has around 35 000 members. Financially, the organization relies on the fees from the members.

Read more about Tanzania League of the Blind (TLB) on Facebook

Tanzania Albinism Society (TAS)

Tanzania has the highest population of people with Albinism in the world, according to some estimates as many as 170,000. TAS has 13,000 members and strives to emancipate people with albinism and their families from society's negative attitudes, discrimination and other barriers that hinder them from enjoying human rights on an equal basis.
