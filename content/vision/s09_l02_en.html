A white cane is a major mobility aid for people with visual impairment. Many people with visual impairment use it as a mobility tool.

The first person that used a white cane was James Biggs. He was a British photographer who became blind after an accident. To be more visible, he painted his walking stick white.



Musa, who is a blind learner at Kyambogo University, sits in a resource room and tells how he learned to move around on campus using a white cane.

He says: "I’ve got a guide. The university employs a guide for me. So, this guide started moving me around this university. So, I started to capture the places, I knew this was the resource room. But my first place to capture was the dining hall actually. Because it is very important to always have meals. So, when I wanted to go to the dining hall, the library or the lecture rooms, the guide could show it to me. They showed me all the places I needed to go, and I slowly started to capture you know, when you stay in a place for a long time you get a mental picture. And also by the course that I did in mobility. So, it gave me a lot of knowledge".



Musa leaves the resource room and let the white cane explore the surface in front of him. He continues moving fast on an outdoor corridor. He seems to know exactly where to go. He says: "So, I started to move alone. I was like…no, I don't want to move with a guide. I want to try moving alone. So, I started to move alone. Taking myself to lectures. Taking myself to the shop". Musa turns to the left when he recognizes a corner with his cane.

He says: "Taking myself to the dining hall and all other places of importance".

He continues walking and reaches some stairs. A new corner is in front of him and Musa turns right on his way towards a shop.

He reaches the shop and says: "Hello. Can I have a soda?"

A shopkeeper gives Musa a soda.

When receiving the bottle Musa says: "Thank you."

Afterwards Musa follow the same route back to the resource room.

Guides

you will find people being guided by others, who are commonly referred to as sighted guides. Guide dogs are trained and can navigate various obstacles. However, the challenge is that the guide dogs cannot interpret street signs, including traffic lights. Furthermore, guide dogs are restricted by some religious organizations. Therefore, it seems difficult for people with visual impairment to be assisted by a guide dog.

In the African context the use of guide dogs is minimal, it also has negative connotation. People are more comfortable when assisted by their friends and family members especially children for social reasons.


But is the use of children as guides problematic?

Listen to Francis Kanubis opinion.
He is the headteacher at Salama School for the Blind, Uganda.

On the issue of the human guides, unfortunately most blind people use human guides as I said. Many of us use our wives, maybe blind women also use their husbands, sighted husbands. Where there are no sighted husbands, where there are no sighted wives, we use our sighted children. I have children who are sighted. So now when I want to go to market, that day the child will not go to school. That child has to stay at home to guide me to the market. May be to hospital, maybe to town, maybe for a meeting. So eventually this child starts performing very badly at school because I used him every day to accompany me somewhere.
Secondly, we are mature people, we are big men, we are big women we use our children even during privacy. For example, I want to go to a toilet, my sighted child is going to guide me to a toilet where I could go alone using my white cane or using a guide dog if we were using guide dogs. Instead of the child accompanying me to the toilet. For example, I want to befriend prossy? as a girl as a lady. I use my own child for a social evening. I want to go to a bar, I want to go to some social life, I want to go for a dance. I go with a child! And eventually, the child learns behaviour which is not good for children. It is child abuse, in other words. That is child abuse. But worst of all, the child’s rights are completely deprived. When the child is supposed to go to play, you are taking him to a bar to accompany you. When the child is supposed to go and read his books, you are taking the child for a dance. You know. So, there are so many inconveniences in depending on children guides.
