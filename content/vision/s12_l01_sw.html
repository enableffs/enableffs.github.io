Profession training

Every individual has a dream of what they want to be. It is not exceptional to persons with visual impairment. They also have their dreams in life- they aspire to be lawyers, journalists, school teachers and university lecturers, just to mention a few. The most powerful tool to enable individuals to achieve their dreams is providing them with quality education.
In East Africa, as it is in other many other countries, there are joint efforts to support provision of education to persons with visual impairment. Due to various reasons including poverty and social barriers, students with visual impairment face barriers to equal learning opportunities, which may curtail their professional dreams in life.
The challenges of accessibility can be of two forms: first, access to education systems and secondly, access to curriculum content for learning.
Educational institutions which may enroll students with visual impairment are limited. Hence, many of them may have limited access to higher education.

Teaching students with visual impairment requires pedagogical skills and appropriate technology to facilitate learning. Likewise, the students with visual impairment need appropriate supportive technology and facilities to enhance learning. Otherwise, limited access to learning may occur regardless of physical access to educational systems. Students with visual impairment experience difficulties in learning sciences, mathematics and any subjects which makes use of graphics representation due to lack of appropriate supportive teaching and learning resources.

Appropriate teaching and learning resources for enhancing learning, include:

  • Braillers
  • Braille embossers
  • Computers with installed speech software
  • Adaptive keyboards
  • Screen readers
  • Talking scientific calculators
  • Optical devices
