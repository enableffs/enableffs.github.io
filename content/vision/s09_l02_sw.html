Fimbo nyeupe ni kisaidizi kikubwa katika kutembea huku na kule kwa ajili ya watu wenye ulemavu wa kuona. Watu wengi wenye ulemavu wa macho hutumia fimbo nyeupe ikiwa ni zana inayosaidia kutembelea sehemu mbalimbali.

Mtu wa kwanza aliyetumia fimbo nyeupe alikuwa anaitwa James Biggs. Alikuwa mpigapicha wa Kiingereza aliyepata ulemavu wa kuona baada ya ajali. Ili aweze kuonekana zaidi alipakaa rangi nyeupe fimbo yake ya kutembelea.

Moses, who is a blind student at Kyambogo University, sits in a resource room and tells how he learned to move around on campus using a white cane.

He says: I’ve got a guide. The university employs a guide for you. So, this guide started moving me around this university. So, I started to capture the places, I knew this was the resource room. But my first place to capture was the dining hall actually. Because it is very important to always have meals. So, when I wanted to go to the dining hall, the library or the lecture rooms, the guide could show it to me. They showed me all the places I needed to go, and I slowly started to capture you know, when you stay in a place for a long time you get a mental picture. And also by the course that I did have in mobility. So, it gave me a lot of knowledge.

Moses leave the resource room and let the white cane explore the surface in front him. He continues moving fast on an outdoor corridor. He seems to know exactly where to go.

He says: So, I started to move alone. I was like…no, I don't want to move with a guide. I want to try moving alone. So, I started to move alone. Taking myself to lectures. Taking myself to the shop. Moses turn to the left when he recognizes a corner with his cane.

He says: Taking myself to the dining hall and all other places of importance.

He continues walking and reaches some stairs. A new corner is in front of him and Moses turns right on his way towards a shop.

He reaches the shop and says: Hello. Can I have a soda?

A saleswoman gives Moses a soda.

When receiving the bottle Moses says: Thank you.

Afterwards Moses follow the same route back to the resource room.

Waongozaji wa njia

Baadhi ya watu huwatumia mbwa maalum mwenye kuelekeza njia badala ya fimbo nyeupe. Mbwa wanaoelekeza njia wamepata mafunzo na wanaweza kuongoza njia kupita kwenye vikwazo mbalimbali. Hatahivyo changamoto ni kwamba mbwa mwongoza njia hawezi kufafanua alama zilizoko mitaani, ikiwa ni pamoja na taa zinazoongoza magari. Zaidi ya hayo, mbwa waongozao watu wanakatazwa kwa baadhi ya mashirika ya dini. Kwahiyo, inaonekana kuwa vigumu kwa watu wenye ulemavu wa kuona kusaidiwa na mbwa.

Katika mazingira ya Kiafrika matumizi ya mbwa mwongoza njia ni madogo, na pia haichukuliwi kwa mtazamo chanya. Watu wanafarijika zaidi wanaposaidiwa na marafiki zao na ndugu zao kwa sababu za kijamii.


Je, kuwatumia watoto kama waongoza njia ni njia isiyokuwa na matatizo?

Sikiliza maoni ya Francis Kanubi.
Yeye ni Mkuu wa Shule ya Wasioona ya Salama.

On the issue of the human rights, unfortunately most blind people use human guides as I said. Many of us use our wives, maybe blind women also use their husbands, sighted husbands. Where there are no sighted husbands, where there are no sighted wives, we use our sighted children. I have children who are sighted. So now when I want to go to market, that day the child will not go to school. That child has to stay at home to guide me to the market. May be to hospital, maybe to town, maybe for a meeting. So eventually this child starts performing very badly at school because I used him every day to accompany me somewhere.
Secondly, we are mature people, we are big man, we are big women we use our children even during privacy. For example, I want to go to a toilet, my sighted child is going to guide me to a toilet where I could go alone using my white cane or using a guide dog if we were using guide dogs. Instead of the child accompanying me to the toilet. For example, I want to to be friend prossy? As a girl as a lady. I use my own child for a social evening. I want to go to a bar, I want to go to some social life, I want to go for a dance. I go with a child! And eventually, the child learns behavior which are not good for children. It is child abuse, in other words. That is child abuse. But worst of all, the child’s rights are completely deprived. When the child is supposed to go to play, you are taking him to a bar to accompany you. When the child is supposed to go and read his books, you are taking the child for a dance. You know. So, there are so many i nconveniences in depending on children guides.
