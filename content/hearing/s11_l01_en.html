Career Choices for children with hearing impairment

School-leavers who are deaf or hard of hearing (D/HH) face additional challenges in their transition to employment. They need career guidance that includes issues related to their hearing loss. D/HH need to be able to advocate for their own careers.

The ability to be an effective self-advocate requires the following:

  • Understanding their own hearing loss and strategies to address their communication challenges
  • Awareness of appropriate accommodations in workplace settings
  • Knowing how and when to disclose their hearing loss
  • Ability to communicate and explain their hearing challenges, accommodations, and solutions
Persons with hearing impairment can take up any of the following career areas:
  • Teaching and training e.g. in sign language and other subject areas
  • Social work and community development work
  • Medical practitioner in areas such as audiologists, dentists, nurses, pharmacists, physicians, psychologists, veterinarians, physical or respiratory therapists, emergency personnel and physician's assistants
  • Engineering works
  • Agriculture, veterinary, horticulture and fisheries skill
  • Entrepreneurial skills within art and industrial design, home economics etc

Vocational training for deaf women and girls

Outside the building two woman are producing liquid soap. From a big tub, they fill small plastic cans with the soap using a spoon. Afterwards they clean the plastic can by removing the soap which remains. A woman enters the compound and buys a plastic can with soap. They communicate in sign language.

In a little dark house a woman is picking mushrooms from plastic bags filled with soil. The plastic bags are hanging in ropes from the roof. The woman leaves the little house. Outside the main building she sits at a table filling small plastic bags with dried mushrooms. She closes the plastic bags by melting the plastic with a candle. Afterwards the bag with mushrooms is put in a basket filled with similar bags.

A woman is sewing decorative, metallic buttons on a piece of textile. Another woman is instructed in sign language by the first woman to thread pearls. They are both creating ornaments.]

United Deaf Women Organisation (UDEWO) is championing the fight against marginalization of deaf women through various initiatives. One of the initiatives is to develop income generating activities. Among the small businesses are knitting sweaters, stockings and scarfs, creating ornaments, making liquid soap and cultivating mushrooms. These products are sold to neighbours and at the local market.
