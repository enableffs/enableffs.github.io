Kama mama au baba hawaongei kwa watoto, je, ni nani atakaye fanya hivyo?



Nancy K. Muwangala,
Lecturer, Mhadhiri na Mtu anayejitolea, Chuo Kikuu cha Kyambogo

Nancy Katumba Muwangala volunteers to teach deaf children about their sexual health.

A female teacher holds a poster with illustrations of the male and female genitals towards lower secondary students who are hearing impaired. The students giggle and laugh. The teacher shows a new poster which illustrate the physical changes in girls. She walks around in the classroom so the students can take a closer look at the poster. The students, both girls and boys, laugh.

In a half-close picture the teacher says: You saw their excitement when I was showing them these pictures. They don´t know these things. They think they are private. But they still should be free with their private life. So, you can see that parents may not have the ability to communicate such issues. Because parents even find it difficult to communicate with their hearing children. Such issues are sensitive.

The teacher communicates to the students using sign language. She is pointing at a girl who goes towards the blackboard. The teacher says:
Whose parents has explained to you about these issues?

Nobody answer. The teacher continues:
So, she's the only one whose parents has talked to her about sexual health. The girl tells her experience to the other students using sign language. The teacher interprets the signs to oral speech: Her mother has told her all that when she was a child. She's now understanding everything about maturity. And she knows how to behave. Her mother told her this because she wanted to protect her from sexual harassment. She could get raped if she's not careful.

When the girl has told her experience the teacher and the students applaud in a silent way waving their arms.

In a half-close picture the teacher says: If the mother or father don't talk to their children, who will? So, if a parent is shy talking about sexual issues with a child whom they can communicate with, what about the deaf child? It becomes so difficult. So, they leave them, they tell their siblings to tell them. And you can never know. They could get wrong information.

The teacher has a conversation in sign language with a girl. Off-screen she says: So that’s why I've done this voluntary counseling whenever they approach me. They get problems as growing girls. A few boys as well. But there are many girls.

Taarifa kuhusu masuala ya ngono kwa watoto ambao ni viziwi.

Wengi wa watoto wenye uziwi hawapati taarifa hii kutoka kwa wazazi wao. Wazazi wengi wanatahayari kuzungumza kuhusu masuala haya. Na hata kama wanataka kuongea kuhusu jambo hilo, hawawezi kujua namna ya kuwasiliana na mtoto wao ambaye ni kiziwi. Hali hii inaleta ugumu kwa watoto ambao ni viziwi kujifunza namna ya kujiheshimu au kuwa na mwenendo mzuri katika masuala ya ngono.

Bila ya kuwa na maarifa kuhusu ngono na mipaka yake wanaweza kuangukia mikononi mwa wabakaji kwa urahisi. Watoto viziwi wanaobakwa pia wana wakati mgumu kuwafafanulia wazazi wao na hata polisi kuhusu nini walichokishuhudia. Na hata kama wana uwezo wa kufafanua. Mara nyingi polisi hawachukulii kwa udhati masuala haya. Hili ni tatizo kubwa, lakini kupata elimu sahihi kunaweza kuwafanya watoto viziwi kuandaliwa zaidi na kuwa na uwezo wa kueleza iwapo kuna jambo litakalomtokea.

Parents should provide sexuality education to their children or engage someone to do it on their behalf.
