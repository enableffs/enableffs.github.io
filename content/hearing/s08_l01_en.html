Adaptive skills for children with hearing impairment

Adaptive skills are social and practical skills that individuals with hearing impairment should learn. They use them in their daily living safely, efficiently, and as independently as possible.Â  Adaptive skills may include:

  • personal care
  • organizational skills
  • writing, reading and numeracy skills
  • laundry and clothing care and hygiene
  • home care and cookery
  • social interaction skills
  • using community services e.g. public transport
  • health and safety
  • information communication and technology
  • recreation and leisure skills
  • time management and budgeting

To manage daily life, children with hearing impairment may need assistive devices and technologies such as hearing aids and cochlea implants.

Hearing aids

Hearing aids are sophisticated audio amplifiers which, to some extent, can compensate for hearing loss. There are many types of hearing aids, some worn on the body, others placed in the ear canal, while others placed behind the ear.

Read more about different types of hearing aids.

The hearing aid amplifies all sounds, but emphasizes the sounds that the user has difficulty perceiving. However the sounds emanating from the hearing aid is not as clearl as the sounds a person with normal hearing experience. Therefore, hearing aid users should get used to the sound image from the instrument and learn how to interpret this.

Since hearing loss is different and is compensated differently, the sound experienced will be different for each indidual.

How hearing aids are used?

Since hearing loss is individual, a hearing aid must be adapted to each user. Even if a hearing aid will not provide the normal hearing function, many "Hard of hearing"- people will have great need of a hearing aid. Since the user of a hearing aid must learn to listen in a new way, it takes time to adjust to using a hearing aid. A hearing aid will amplify sounds which she/he has not heard in a long time or maybe never heard. In the beginning, it is recommended that the person become familiar with the daily sounds and where they come from. Initially, the sounds could be heard unfamiliar, but the user will gradually get used to them.

By talking or reading aloud to himself one becomes accustomed to his/her own voice. Eventually the person needs to go to quiet places, outside the home - a park or forest and listen to the sounds there. Places with background noise should be avoided, for it can be experienced very noisy at first.

About 20% of people with hearing loss would benefit from devices such as hearing aids and cochlear implants

Basic guide to using a hearing aid

  • ensure that the ear mould is properly fitting in your ear
  • switch on the hearing aid and ensure that the volume is properly adjusted before use
  • keep the hearing aid clean at all times
  • avoid making your hearing aid wet as this will damage it
  • ensure that the battery is fully charged

Tips for conversations with others

  • Initially use hearing aid when talking to a person you are familiar with.
  • Let the conversation take place in a quiet place.
  • Tell the other person you are wearing a hearing aid and ask the other to talk in normal tone of voice.
  • It is important that the face of the speaker is seen.
  • Eventually you can try to participate in conversations under more noisy conditions. Then you will be able to find out in which situationst the hearing aid works best.
