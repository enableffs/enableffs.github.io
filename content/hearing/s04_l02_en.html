For children with hearing impairment to become mature, self-reliant, respected members of the community, vocational training, besides education, is vital.

Vocational training for children with hearing impairment

Vocational skills enable young people with hearing impairment to compete successfully for employment opportunities, be financially independent and self-reliant. Self-reliance plays a key role in raising awareness and securing equal rights for persons with hearing impairment.

Vocational training for persons with hearing impairment includes trades such as: weaving, catering /cookery, sewing and embroidery; carpentry, car mechanics and metal work; electronics and electrical installation, videography, music, dance and drama.

The Uganda School for the Deaf offers primary education with pre-vocation skills in carpentry, tailoring, home sciences, and agricultural practice for students who don't fit well in the academic classes.
One intention of the pre-vocation training is to give them skills for further vocational training so the students can get employment in the future.
