What is communication?

Communication means to share something; emotions, expressions, ideas and memories. It happens between two or more partners where the partners are active in the process and together they co-create or develop commiunication.

Humans are social beings and communication with others is an essential and important aspect of our lives.

The ability to communicate is essential to convey our opinions, needs and feelings. Through communication we create social relations and make us participate in the life that surround us.
Communication difficulties appear primarily when the hearing is impaired. If the sight is reduced too, the ability to further communication decreases.

What is communication used for?

  • Obtaining personal needs.
    This is the most basic way of communication.
  • Informing about what has already happened, what is happening and what is going to happen.
  • Commenting on the interest of the deafblind person.
  • Sharing experiences in a conversation.
    To communicate about what you experience together is important for for developing self-esteem and for developing communication further.

Why communication is the main focus in deafblind services

Communication is the main goal because it includes emotional, social and cognitive development. Communication is important because it:

  • Create bonding with parents, family members, houseparents and teachers.
  • Promotes social interaction. The deafblind child learns to establish and sustain contact and social rules like turn-taking.
  • Support the development of self-esteem. The child feels she can master things on her own, she is understood and appreciated by her partner and she can influence her own life.
  • Building relationships and awareness of other persons. It is the precondition for developing empathy.
  • Understand the physical and social surroundings. Building up mental images and concepts of what they experience.

Main methods of communication

Deaf-blind people have many different ways of communication. The methods they use vary, depending on the causes of their combined vision and hearing loss, their backgrounds, and their education.

The main methods of communication with a deafblind person or between two deafblind people are:

  • Tactile Sign Language
    The deaf-blind person puts his or her hands over the signer’s hands to feel the shape, movement and location of the signs.
  • Tactile Fingerspelling
    The deaf-blind person may prefer to put his or her hand over the fingerspelling hand, or on the signer’s palm, or cup his or her hand around the signer’s hand. Usually people who have been deaflind prefer tactile fingerspelling because sometimes sign language can be difficult to learn.
  • Screen Braille Communicator (digital)
  • Haptic communication.
    The idea behind the haptic communication is to share visual and auditory information from the environment, using the haptic signals in parallel with the ordinary communication. It is meant to serve as a complement to the spoken or signed language in a communication situation.
    Haptic communication does not replace speech or sign language. It is about creating meaning and context, to get a better picture of the situation and the room you are in.
    It will strengthen and complement other kinds of communication between human beings.
    Haptic communication takes place through the body contact at neutral zones in the body like back, shoulder, hand, knee or foot. It is bounded to the situation, context, place and orientation.

    Haptic communication can be used to:

    • Feedback and communication
    • Describe of colours
    • Characterizing and feelings
    • Mobility
    • Describe food and drink
    • Describe a room
    • Map the surroundings
    • Tell what´s happening?
    • Tell the weather
  • Tadoma
    This is a way for deaf-blind people with little or no usable vision to speechread another person by touch. They put their thumb on the other person’s chin, and their fingers on the other person’s cheek to feel the vibrations of the person’s voice and the movement of their lips.

Each individual is different

To maintain the contact with others, sounds residues needs to be recovered as far as possible. Even if both hearing and vision are lost, the communication with others can be maintained. Be aware of each individual is different and requires individual adaptations and communication methods.

Joy of life

Tips for communicating with people who are deaf-blind:

  • Make sure you have the person’s attention before trying to communicate with her/him.
  • Gently touching the top of the person’s arm and identify yourself clearly.
  • Keep in physical contact during the conversation.
  • Speak slowly and clearly in short sentences, but don't shout.
  • Be clear about the theme you're talking about and refer to known references.
  • Use gestures and facial expressions to support what you are saying.
  • Speak one at a time.
  • Avoid background noise.
  • Use the time needed for the conversation.
  • Always reply back.
  • Repeat phrases or re-phrase the sentence if necessary.
  • Talk face to face.
  • Keep your face visible and be sure it is lightened.
