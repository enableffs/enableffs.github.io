There have been several efforts globally and regionally about how to advocate the right of people with visual impairment.

The Universal Declaration for Human Rights adopted by United Nations emphasizes the human rights and anti-discriminations among people..

Therefore, people with visual impairment among others have equal rights before the law. People with visual impairment therefore have rights to:

  • education
  • life
  • recognition
  • freedom of opinion and expression
  • marry
  • own properties
  • work

These rights are internationally declared.


In each country there should policies which put these human rights into legal practices. All constitutions in Kenya, Tanzania and Uganda emphasized the rights of people with disability. Furthermore, there are policies and act addressing the issue of people with disability.
