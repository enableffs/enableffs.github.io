Vision can simply refer to ability to see which is enabled by the sensory system. Sensory system is a part of the nervous system responsible for processing sensory information. What the eye sees is what we refer to as sensory information.

In the process of see the information is processed through a visual system which includes:

  • the sense organ (eye)
  • the receptors-rods and cones
  • the whole process of visual pathways which starts from eye to the brain.

Courtesy rules of blindness

  1. He/she is an ordinary person – just blind
    • You do not need to raise your voice
    • Do not address him/ her as if to a child
    • Do not ask other people question that he/she could answer e.g what does he want, what is her name?
  2. He may use a long cane to walk independently; he may also take your arm for sighted guidance.
    • Let her decide for herself
    • Do not grab her arm let her take your instead
    • He will take a help step behind you to anticipate Potholes, steps and other barriers
    • Guide him to a chair by placing his palm on the back or seat of the chair.
  3. Announce you presence whenever you enter or leave a room where there is a blind people.
    • Speak when you enter
    • Introduce him to others including children
    • Let him know if there is a cat or dog in the house or elsewhere
    • Prompt him whenever extending a hand for greetings
  4. Any door left partially open is a hazard to the blind person
    • Rooms, windows, cabinets, drawers should be left either completely open or completely closed
  5. They have no problems with ordinary table skills
  6. Do not avoid words like ”see”, she is always gld to see you
  7. Do not pity him – but avoid talking about the ”wonderful compensations” of blindness.
    • The sense of smell, touch and/or hearing did not improve when blindness stuck – He relies on them more and therefore thy may provide more information to him than to you.
  8. Always familiarize a blind person to a new environment. If she is you house guest – show her the bathroom, tables, windows, switches.
  9. If you are curious about blindness ask a few questions. To her its an old story, she has other interests like any other person
  10. Do not think of him as just a blind person, he is just a person who happens to be blind.
  11. You do not have to remember som politically correct terms such as visually impaired or visually challenged – keep it simple and honest, just say blind.
