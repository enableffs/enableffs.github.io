Wherever communities of deaf people exist, sign languages have been developed. A common misconception is that all sign languages are the same worldwide. Each country generally has its own, native sign language, and some have more than one. Inside Kenya there are some dialect differences between Kisumu and Mombasa. At the same time, sign languages may share similarities to each other.

Introducing sign language to preschool-children

First the children imitate the teachers movement and copy them. Then they learn new concepts and signs through fysical acticities. New signs are presented in different representations; as physical objects, as drawings and as written words.
