In Uganda 90 % of deaf children are not offered eduaction.
How will their future be?
The society becomes more and more independent of citizens who are well educated and hearing impaired citizens are a resource for the community which can be taken into use. The education of hearing impaired children can be done in two ways, either in a special school or the students can be included in an ordinary class together with hearing students. There are advantages and disadvantages to both education systems.

Special schools for the deaf students

Students learn sign language as their mother tongue

At Uganda school for the Deaf the pupils learn sign language as their mother tongue and english or other as their foreign language. It is impossible to use sign language with spoken language simultaneously; that is why sign language is still a challenge to the learners', education and parents'.

Evaluation of deaf students

The current National Primary Leaving Examination in Uganda demands the Deaf to be fluent in English before they are graded in a given subject, which situation ignores the fact that, learning any second language is influenced by ones mother tongue.

In their examination regulations "The Uganda National Examinations Board" allows deaf candidates to be guided by sign language interpreters and readers who are expected to tape record candidates responses.

Nevertheless, as a result, majority of the deaf adolescents end up failing exams and eventually dropping out of school.

Inclusion of students who are hard of hearing in an ordinary class

If there is a student who is hard of hearing in your class, you should be aware of these adaptions of the classroom environment:

  • The sitting arrangement
  • Put light on the face of the person who talks, backlight should be avoided
  • Be shure you get the child´s attention
  • Adjust your vocabulary
  • Show your face
  • Use various communication modes
  • The students should speak one at a time
