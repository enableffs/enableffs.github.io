Education of the Deaf is now a right in Uganda, but only 10% of the hearing impaired children are enrolled for the primary education.

The connection between sign language and English

Sign language is a visual and gestural language based on the use of hands, eyes, face, mouth and body. It is visual language expressed with eye movement as well as facial expression. The pupils at the school learn sign language as their mother tongue and english or other as their foreign language. It is impossible to use sign language with spoken language simultaneously; that is why sign language is still a challenge to the learners', education and parents'.Among others, one reason can be lack of awareness and sensitization on Deafness within the community

Evaluation of deaf students

The current National Primary Leaving Examination demands the Deaf to be fluent in English before they are graded in a given subject, which situation ignores the fact that, learning any second language is influenced by ones mother tongue. As a result, majority of the Deaf adolescents end up failing exams and eventually dropping out of school.
